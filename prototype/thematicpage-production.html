Mineral production is the process of extracting ore or minerals from the ground. During the exploration phase, companies typically engage in prospecting to identify potential deposits. Once located, the reserves must be modelled by the company and certified by an accredited agency before application for a production license. Once the required studies (such as environmental impact assessments, feasibility studies, etc.) are completed and the mine is developed, commercial production commences. EITI countries disclose figures on volumes of production, exports and their market value.

Production Figures

Production figures refer to the volumes or quantity of mineral production. They are typically reported in tonnes for most bulk commodities (such as copper and coal), in ounces for precious metals (such as gold and silver), in barrels of oil equivalent for petroleum products (such as crude oil, condensate and natural gas) and in carat for gemstones. While different regions and companies sometimes report production volumes in different units (for instance using the imperial system), production volume figures can be converted into similar units for ease of comparison.

Other Sources for Production Data

While the EITI Reports provide production data, there are also a number of other sources for this data. These include the national mining or oil and gas regulator, the BP Statistical Review, the US Geological Service, the US Energy Information Administration, the International Energy Asgency, the World Bank and the various international mining associations (eg: World Gold Council, World Nuclear Association, etc.).

Understanding Production Figures

Production figures, in both volume and value, are typically provided as annual aggregates. Daily average production figures are available by dividing annual production by 365. Fluctuations in benchmark commodity prices can lead to greater fluctuations in the market value of production than in volumes.

What the EITI standard requires (link to the implementers guide)

The EITI Standard requires that countries disclose the volume and market value of production and exports for each of the mineral commodities produced. This data should be provided by state and region where relevant.

EITI Reports are important for tracking the performance of each company in terms of compliance with their obligations towards state revenue. Detailed reporting will enable better analysis and comparisons with other data sources which will hopefully shed light on potential lost revenue and give policy recommendations to improve extractive industry governance.
